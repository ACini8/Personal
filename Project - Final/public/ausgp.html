Round 1 - Melbourne

Andre Cini 9th of March 2026

The 2026 Australian Grand Prix introduced a brand-new era of Formula 1 with sweeping technical regulations for both chassis and power units. Here is the breakdown of how the season opener at Albert Park unfolded.

Free Practice: The New Era Breaks Cover

The three practice sessions were a mix of "new car" teething issues and raw pace demonstration.

  • FP1: Ferrari signaled they were ready to fight, with Charles Leclerc leading Lewis Hamilton for a Scuderia 1-2. Reigning champion Lando Norris suffered early gearbox issues, while rookie Arvid Lindblad turned heads by finishing P5.
  • FP2: Local hero Oscar Piastri topped the timesheets, giving the home crowd hope. However, the session was messy: Max Verstappen damaged his floor in a gravel trip, and George Russell had a pit-lane brush with Lindblad.
  • FP3: Mercedes finally showed their hand as George Russell set the fastest time of the weekend. His teammate, Kimi Antonelli, caused a minor red flag after his car dropped cooling devices onto the track, though he recovered to finish the session.

Qualifying: Russell Reigns, Verstappen Recedes

The season couldn't wait till the race to provide some shocks of 2026 as Qualifying brought some early surprises:

  • Q1 Drama: In a massive upset, 4-Time World Champion Max Verstappen crashed out at Turn 1 on his first flying lap after a rear-axle lock, leaving him P20 on the grid. He was joined on the sidelines by Carlos Sainz and Lance Stroll, whose teams couldn't get their cars ready in time.
  • Mercedes Dominance: George Russell was untouchable, topping every segment. with the closest non-mercedes driver being almost 8 tenths off pole position
  • New Car, No Problem: Red Bull driver Isack Hadjar stunned the paddock when he put his Red Bull in P3, after getting the move to the senior team, splitting the Ferrari and the Mercedes heavyweights. was certainly a great start to his sophmore season.

Top 5 Classification:

  1. George Russell - Mercedes
  2. Andrea Kimi Antonelli - Mercedes
  3. Isack Hadjar - Red Bull Racing
  4. Charles Leclerc - Scuderia Ferrari
  5. Lewis Hamilton - Scuderia Ferrari

Race Recap: Mercedes back to Domination

The race was defined by a strategic split between Mercedes and Ferrari with the opening race being one to forget for a few of our drivers.

The Start & Early Scraps

Before the race even begun, heartbreak struck before the lights went out as Oscar Piastri crashed on his way to the grid, ending his home race prematurely. When the race began, Charles Leclerc had a "rocket ship" start from P4, surging into the lead by the first corner. The opening 10 laps were a breathless battle; the lead swapped between Leclerc and Russell seven times as they experimented with the new 2026 energy deployment systems

Race Defining Moment

On Lap 11, Isack Hadjar's dream debut ended in smoke as his RB22 suffered a power unit failure which triggered the Virtual Safety Car.

  • The Mercedes Gamble: Both Russell and Antonelli dived into the pits for Hard tires. pulling off the double stack and conceding the lead of the race.
  • The Ferrari Choice: Ferrari opted to stay out, gambling on track position and a later stop due to the chaotic nature of the opening race of the season.

This proved to be the race-defining error for Ferrari. While Leclerc and Hamilton led on track, the Mercedes duo utilized their fresh rubber to carve through the field. By the time the Ferraris finally pitted on Lap 26, Russell had built enough of a gap to stay comfortably ahead.

The Closing Stages

Once regaining the lead, Mercedes switched to "Management Mode", holding a steady yet dominant 15-second gap over the Ferrari's as George Russell won the season opener in Melbourne firmly stamping Mercedes as the team to beat. Behind them Lando Norris had a very quiet race in his first as World Champion, meanwhile his rival from last year, Max Verstappen had a now signature comeback drive climbing all the way to P6 after starting P20. Rookie sensation Arvid Lindblad secured points on debut for Racing Bulls with a respectable P8, whilst Gabriel Bortoleto scored Audi their first points in Formula 1

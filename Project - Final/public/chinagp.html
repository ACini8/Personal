Round 2 - Shanghai

Andre Cini 16th of March 2026

The 2026 Chinese Grand Prix in Shanghai was a historic weekend that officially confirmed the "changing of the guard" in Formula 1. As the first Sprint weekend of the new regulations era, it was a high-intensity affair dominated by Mercedes, but defined by the rise of a new superstar

Free Practice & Sprint: Mercedes Asserts Control

With only one practice session (FP1) due to the Sprint format, teams were under immense pressure to master the 2026 active aerodynamics on Shanghai's long straights

  • FP1: George Russell led a Mercedes 1-2, showing that the Silver Arrows had the most stable platform for the high-speed "snail" turns.
  • The Sprint: George russell took a commanding victory on Saturday. However, his teammate Kimi Antonelli struggled, picking up a 10-second penalty for a collision with fellow sophomore driver Isack Hadjar and finishing a disappointing P5.

Qualifying: A Historic Milestone

Qualifying was a rollercoaster that saw the record books rewritten.

  • The Russell Scare: George Russell looked to be cruising to pole until a reliability glitch in Q3 forced hum ti abandon his first run. He sat P10 with minutes to go.
  • Amazing Antonelli: Whilst Russell was in the garage, his much younger teammate Kimi Antonelli set a blistering lap time of 1:32.064 setting the bar quite high for the other 9 to beat.
  • The Result: Russell managed to get back out there and salvage P2, but he couldn't beat his teammate, no one could as Antonelli became the youngest pole-sitter in F1 history, breaking a record that has stood for 12 years, set by none other than Sebastian Vettel.

Top 5 Classification:

  1. Andrea Kimi Antonelli - Mercedes
  2. George Russell - Mercedes
  3. Lewis Hamilton - Ferrari
  4. Charles Leclerc - Ferrari
  5. Oscar Piastri - McLaren

Race Recap: Antonelli's Redemption

The Grand Prix had everything, redemption stories, technical brilliance, great drives and utter nightmares.

The McLaren Meltdown

The biggest shock occurred before the lights went out. Reigning World Champion Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri both suffered terminal technical problems. For the first time in years, both McLarens were DNS (Did Not Start), leaving two gaping holes at the front of the grid

Race Highlights & Major Incidents

  • The Start: Antonelli lost the lead to Russell off the line but stayed glued to his gearbox. On Lap 2, the teenager made a bold lunging move down the inside of the back straight to reclaim P1. Further back, Isack Hadjar had a spin before the back straight nearly taking out Oliver Bearman in the process, thankfully both drivers finished the race.
  • Dueling Ferraris: By far the best battle was between the Ferrari drivers, as Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc had an incredibly close battle throughout the whole race for the final podium position. It was tight yet fair racing which kept everyone on their seats.
  • Verstappen's Woes: Max Verstappen's difficult start to the new campaign goes from bad to worse; After qualifying in a measly P8, he was fighting in the midfield for P6 until an ERS cooling failure forced him to retire on Lap 46.

Final Conclusions:

Starting from Pole Position, Kimi Antonelli took his maiden Grand Prix win, marking Italy's first win in F1 since Giancarlo Fisichella in 2006. Teammate George Russell finished in P2 retaining his championship lead, followed by Lewis Hamilton who took his maiden Ferrari podium after joining last year. Haas Driver Oliver Bearman continued his great start to year by finishing a career best P5. Finally, Alpine Driver Franco Colapinto scored his first points for the team in a double points finish as teammate Pierre Gasly came home in an impressive P6, a great day for the Enstone outfit.

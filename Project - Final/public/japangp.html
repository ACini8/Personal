Round 3 - Suzuka

Andre Cini 30th of March 2026

The 2026 Japanese Grand Prix at Suzuka was a masterclass in adaptation, featuring a delayed start, a high-G impact, and a teenage sensation rewriting the history books once again.

Free Practice: Mercedes' Suzuka Stranglehold

The technical "S" Curves of Suzuka provided the ultimate test for the 2026 chassis regulations.

  • FP1 & FP2: Mercedes picked up exactly where they left off in China. Kimi Antonelli and George Russell traded fastest sectors, while McLaren’s Lando Norris saw his Friday ruined by a hydraulic leak and a battery issue.
  • FP3: Antonelli topped the final session by a quarter of a second. Max Verstappen and Red Bull continued to struggle with the new power unit's energy deployment, languishing in P8 and P11, respectively

Qualifying: Silver Front Row

Qualifying confirmed that the Mercedes W17 is currently the class of the field, particularly in high-speed aero-dependency.

  • Q1 & Q2: The big shock was Max Verstappen being eliminated in Q2, he could only manage P11 whilst he was outqualified once again by his teammate Isack Hadjar who qualified P8
  • Q3 Shootout: Kimi Antonelli secured his second consecutive pole position with a 1:28.778, beating teammate George Russell by nearly three-tenths
  • Piastri's Back: After a horrid start to the season Oscar Piastri showed us why he was a championship protagnist last year by qualifying P3, out-qualifying his teammates and both Ferraris.

Top 5 Classification:

  1. Andrea Kimi Antonelli - Mercedes
  2. George Russell - Mercedes
  3. Oscar Piastri - McLaren
  4. Charles Leclerc - Scuderia Ferrari
  5. Lando Norris - McLaren

Race Recap: A Strategic Swing at Suzuka

The race began with a 10-minute delay after a massive crash in a Porsche support race damaged the barriers at Turn 12, requiring urgent repairs, which I am sure gave some of us a bit more sleep.

The Start & Early Battles

When the lights finally went out, Oscar Piastri executed a perfect launch, threading the needle between the two Mercedes to snatch the lead into Turn 1. Behind him, the Mercedes duo struggled for grip, momentarily falling back into the clutches of the Ferraris. By Lap 8, Russell had recovered to P2 and began a frantic wheel-to-wheel duel with Piastri, swapping the lead twice in a single lap between the Casio Triangle and Turn 1.

Bearman's 50G Impact: Race Defining Moment

The race was turned on it's head on Lap 22, when Haas driver Oliver Bearman suffered a high-speed rear-suspension failure heading into Turn 13, results in a violent 50G Impact with the barriers. Luckily, Ollie was safe and hasn't reported any serious injuries, this crash obviously brought out the safety car. This led to race altering results:

  • Unlucky Timing:George Russell and Oscar Piastri had just pitted the lap prior. meaning that they essentially lost position at the worst possible time.
  • Fortune Favors the Bold: Kimi Antonelli stayed out longer and due to this got a "cheap" pit stop and emerge into the lead of the race.

This proved to be the race-defining error for Ferrari. While Leclerc and Hamilton led on track, the Mercedes duo utilized their fresh rubber to carve through the field. By the time the Ferraris finally pitted on Lap 26, Russell had built enough of a gap to stay comfortably ahead.

Final Laps:

Once the race restarted, Antonelli was supreme and bolted away from the field, winning his second race in a row 13.7 seconds clear of the grid and taking the lead of the Drivers Championship, becoming the youngest ever Championship Leader in the process. Oscar Piastri came home to score McLaren a much needed podium in his first race start of the season. The real action was the three-way "slugfest" for the final podium spot between Leclerc, Russell, and Hamilton. However, it was Leclerc eventually secured P3 after a daring move on Russell at the hairpin with three laps to go, whilst his teammate fell down to P6 due to lack of power.

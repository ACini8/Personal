Miami GP - Top Choices

Andre Cini 27th of April 2026

The 2026 season returns after a month long break in the calendar as we head to the sunshine state to go racing in Miami, Florida. The hierarchy is slowly getting more established as the season goes along, however with new technical regulations coming to Miami, with increased Practice times, this weekend may throw us in for a curveball.

Last time out in Miami: Utter Papaya Dominance

Last time out in Miami, it was all McLaren; finishing 1-2 twice in the sprint and the race, with Lando Norris taking the Sprint win on the saturday and Oscar Piastri taking the win on Sunday after an impressive drive from P4. Teams like Williams and Red Bullhad strong weekends with both drivers for them finishing in the points and it was Max Verstappenwho actually started from pole position on the Sunday.

In terms of F1 Fantasy, it was a big hit for our Limitless users with me personally scoring my best total of last season with 414 Points. Here are my Top 3 drivers to potentially look at and to avoid.

Buy and Avoid: Drivers

Drivers to Buy

You have 5 choices, use them wisely as they could make or break your team.

  1. Kimi Antonelli (£24.1M): The kid is a living cheat code; with back to back wins in China and Suzuka, he's leading the standings and his price is still reasonably affordable at £24.1 Million. I would do anything I can if you don't have him to get him in. His car is great, he's in fine form and he needs to score -31 points or less for his budget to go down, which will never happen, certified lock.
  2. Liam Lawson (£7.5M): The Kiwi driver has had a great start to the 2026 F1 season with our projections showing he has a 94% chance of increasing his budget and with the VCARB underneath him looking like a solid midfield car, with some chaos that Miami always seems to bring, he could be a very good option if you want a low budget driver.
  3. Oscar Piastri (£24.6 M): Risky? Yes, but the signs are there for McLaren to have a good weekend. After a disastrous first 2 races for Oscar with two consecutive DNs he bounced back in Suzuka with a P2 finish after fighting for the win showing why he was in the championship picture last year. Last Year he won the race in Miami with a dominant drive, and with the Mercedes power unit behind him on the long straights of Miami, I can see a strong weekend for Oscar and McLaren.

Drivers to Avoid

  1. Max Verstappen (£28.2M): At £28.2 Million, the most expensive driver in the game looks like a poor pick, and if you have him in your team, I would highly recommend cashing on your loses before his price starts dropping. With two poor scoring weekends in Shanghai and Japan, the chances of him having a good weekend on a track like Miami and with the RB22 looking like a midfield car; I just can't see Max or Red Bull having a productive weekend in F1 Fantasy
  2. Carlos Sainz (£12.4M): Williams and Carlos did not have a great Japanese GP starting in 16th and finishing one place above, on a track like Miami. All he needs is 2 points to get an increase in budget and with a sprint weekend that is very possible of course. I just think at his price point it's not worth putting him in your team due to Williams' inconsistencies and also with there being multiple drivers such as Liam Lawson and even Esteban Ocon who look better in form and cheaper.
  3. Gabriel Bortoleto (£5.8M): The Brazilian has a 91% chancethat he does not score enough points to gain a budget, if you have Bortoleto in your team however I would recommend keeping him as if he has a decent 10-12 point weekend, yes his price goes down but he would be a great cheap driver to have over the next 2-3 races.

Buy and Avoid: Constructors

What about the Constructors? They gain the most points in your team so it's crucial to get these right.

Constructors to Buy

  1. Ferrari (£24.2M): Ferrari are bringing major upgrades to the East Coast and with how strong they have started the year giving Mercedes their only real challenge to date, we could see another strong weekend for Ferrari, and with their previous performances they would need to score -107 Pointsto lose budget, so it's fair to say that they are quite safe.
  2. Audi (£5.6M): For me, this is a sneaky pick that people may not think about, they scored 23 points last time out in Japan and with just 2 points they can increase their budget by £0.6 Million. If you've had rough start to your season and need a cheap team, I personally think the German outfit is a great choice at only £5.6M.

Constructors to Avoid

  1. Aston Martin (£8.5M): Do I need to explain myself? With two negative scoring results and with the car not looking any safer to drive they would need an absolute miracle to have a positive weekend in Miami, with our projections not even giving them a 1% chanceto increase their budget over this weekend. Maybe they can kick-start their season here, but I dont see them as a viable option in F1 Fantasy.
  2. Red Bull (£29.1M): If there is one thing we can conclude about this F1 season regarding Red Bull Racing is that their new shiny power unit is not the most reliable. With DNF's in Melbourne and Shanghai and deployment issues in Suzuka, in the hot and humid environments of Miami, I just do not trust the car and it's reliability especially over the course of a sprint weekend.

Chips: Time for Limitless

If you have not used your Limitless chip yet, I highly suggest using it this weekend; with this weekend being the last sprint weekend until The British Grand Prix in 2 months, it is now or never to use the limitless chip as everyone's teams are gonna get more and more expensive rendering limitless useless for further usage. In regards to other possible chips, I think final fix is in play here as it is for all sprint weekends. Apart from those two chips, I think you should save the chips for a later day, it's early on in the season and I don't think using a 3x or no negative is worth it.

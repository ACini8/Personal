Round 4 - Miami

Andre Cini 4th of May 2026

The 2026 Miami Grand Prix was another statement weekend in Formula 1’s new era, as Kimi Antonelli continued his incredible run of form by taking victory at the Miami International Autodrome. However, unlike all the races so far, this was not a straightforward Mercedes domination, as McLaren finally brought a serious fight through Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri.

Free Practice & Sprint: McLaren Strike Back

With the Miami weekend using the Sprint format, teams only had one Free Practice session to understand their upgrades and adapt to the hot and humid conditions.

  • FP1: Charles Leclerc topped the only practice session with a time of 1:29.310, ahead of Max Verstappen and Oscar Piastri. This immediately suggested that Ferrari, Red Bull and McLaren had closed the gap to Mercedes.
  • Mercedes Trouble: The Silver Arrows did not look as comfortable as usual with both Antonelli and Russell facing early issues during practice. Antonelli could only manage P5, which made the weekend look more open than expected
  • The Sprint: Here we saw the full confirmation of the gap being closed as Lando Norris took the win in the Sprint for McLaren, spearheading a 1-2 for the papaya outfit with Charles Leclerc taking the last podium place. Our championship leader had a poor start and dropped to P6 due to track limits with his teammate finishing in P4.

Qualifying: Ant-Man Responds

Qualifying showed us that things in Formula 1 can change very quickly in a matter of hours

  • Kimi's Lap: After a difficult sprint, Kimi Antonelli produced a fantastic lap to take pole position for the Miami GP, making it 3 poles in a row for the young Italian.
  • Super Max: Even with how dysfunctional that RB22 has looked this year, Max Verstappen proved why he's a 4-time champ by qualifying P2 giving Red Bull their best result of the year so far.
  • McLaren Problems: Despite their dominance in the Sprint race, both McLarens struggled more in the afternoon session, with Norris managing P4 whilst Piastri had issues earlier in the session causing him to qualify in a lowly P7.

Top 5 Classification:

  1. Andrea Kimi Antonelli - Mercedes
  2. Max Verstappen - Red Bull
  3. Charles Leclerc - Scuderia Ferrari
  4. Lando Norris - McLaren
  5. George Russell - Mercedes

Race Recap: Change in the pecking order

The Grand Prix was a mix of chaos, strategy, and late drama. Unlike his previous wins, Antonelli had to fight hard for this one, especially against a fast and determined Lando Norris.

The McLaren Threat

The biggest surprise of the weekend was McLaren’s pace. After Norris won the Sprint and Piastri finished second, it was clear that McLaren were not just fighting for podiums anymore, but possibly race wins.

During the race, Norris kept constant pressure on Antonelli and looked like he had the pace to challenge for victory. However, Mercedes made the better strategy call, allowing Antonelli to regain control after the pit stop phase.

Race Highlights & Major Incidents

  • The Start: The race began with a dramatic three-way fight between Antonelli, Verstappen and Leclerc. Both Antonelli and Verstappen locked up into Turn 1, while Verstappen later spun and dropped down the order. This allowed Leclerc to briefly take the lead.
  • Early Safety Car: The opening laps were chaotic, with separate crashes for Isack Hadjar and Pierre Gasly bringing out the Safety Car. This immediately changed the rhythm of the race and forced teams to rethink their strategies.
  • Strategy Battle: The race eventually became a two-driver fight between Antonelli and Norris. Norris had the pace, but Mercedes used the undercut perfectly, allowing Antonelli to come out ahead and defend the lead in the final stint.
  • Piastri's Late Charge: The Australian completed a strong McLaren weekend by snatching the final podium place from Charles Leclerc in the closing stages, giving McLaren a double podium.
  • Leclerc's Disaster: Charles Leclerc looked set for a strong result to keep Ferrari's season smooth sailing, but his race fell apart late on. Whilst chasing Oscar Piastri, he spun and hit the wall, damaging his suspension and losing places in the process. After the race, he was given a 20-second penalty for repeatedly cutting the track, falling behind his teammate who also didn't have a great race.
  • Verstappen's Recovery: Max Verstappen recovered from his early spin to finish P5, which was a respectable result considering how poor his opening lap had been.

Final Conclusions:

Starting from pole position, Kimi Antonelli took yet another impressive win, making it three consecutive Grand Prix victories and further strengthening his championship lead. However, this race was different because Mercedes were not completely dominant. McLaren showed serious improvement, with Lando Norris finishing P2 and Oscar Piastri completing the podium.

For Ferrari, Miami was a missed opportunity. Charles Leclerc had the pace to fight at the front, but mistakes and penalties ruined what could have been a podium finish. Lewis Hamilton had a quieter race but still gained from Leclerc’s penalty.

Further back, Franco Colapinto delivered a strong result for Alpine by finishing P7, while Carlos Sainz and Alex Albon gave Williams a double-points finish. Overall, Miami proved that while Antonelli and Mercedes remain the team to beat, McLaren may now be close enough to turn the championship fight into a real battle.

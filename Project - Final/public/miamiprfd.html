Miami - Post Race Fantasy Debrief

Andre Cini 16th of March 2026

The Miami GP has come and gone, and for F1 Fantasy players, this was one of those weekends that reminded everyone how quickly a recommendation can either look genius or completely fall apart. With the Sprint format adding extra points, extra risk and extra chances for chaos, the 2026 Miami Grand Prix gave us plenty to look back on.

In our Miami Top Choices piece, we gave our recommendations on who to invest in and who to avoid, today we will be looking further into this and seeing where we went right and where we missed the mark.

The Hits:

  • Kimi Antonelli: We said it before and we'll say it again the kid is a cheat code, scoring 42 points and gaining 0.3M in budget, he was a good sprint performance away from the perfect weekend. With 92 points scored in the last 2 races and 3 wins on the bounce, there is no stopping him
  • Oscar Piastri: It was a risk but its fair to say it payed off, Piastri and McLaren looked like their old self this weekend proving to be a viable asset in the future. Oscar scored 41 points this weekend and after a good weekend in Suzuka as well, he looks like a very good option in the next 2-3 races, especially after the McLaren's new performance boost.
  • Red Bull: In our top choices, we told you to steer clear of Red Bull, and whilst they returned an okay 31 points, for their budget point, that is nowhere near good enough for them, so our projections were correct. With 2 low scoring weekends in a row, and still being the 2nd most expensive car whilst being 4th in the constructors, they're just not reliable enough especially with their inconsistencies.
  • Ferrari: were recommended as a constructor to buy, but this was a mixed call at best. They still scored in both the Sprint and the race, but compared to McLaren and Mercedes, it was a disappointing weekend. Ferrari scored 57 fantasy points, while McLaren scored 110 and Mercedes scored 104. However with them still being valued at only 24.5M I wouldn't give up on Ferrari just yet.

The Misses:

Unfortunately, due to the chaotic nature of this weekend, their were more misses then hits on our end, which we apologize for, whether it was recommendations that went wrong, our drivers and teams that proved us wrong.

  • Max Verstappen: "I just can't see Max or Red Bull having a productive weekend in F1 Fantasy", I wonder which idiot said that? The dutchmman scored the 2nd most points out of any driver this weekend with a staggering 52 points firmly shutting the critics up.
  • Liam Lawson: The Kiwi driver unfortunately did not work out as expected this weekend. After his collision with Pierre Gasly, Lawson sustained irreversible damage to the car and was forced to retire from the race scoring a negative result in F1 Fantasy.
  • Aston Martin: I can't believe I am saying this, but Aston Martin had a solid and positive weekend. They scored a whopping 38 points (more than Red Bull!). Out of all the things said in the previous article, I am glad to say I got this one wrong, although they shouldn't be on anyone's investments list yet as they still had a negative return in Suzuka.

What to expect moving forward

After Miami, the biggest fantasy takeaway is simple: McLaren are back in the conversation. Their double podium in the Sprint and Grand Prix shows that they are no longer just a good option, but possibly one of the best constructor choices going forward.

Antonelli remains one of the safest premium drivers in the game, Piastri is becoming more tempting, and Verstappen has reminded everyone that even when Red Bull are not winning, he can still produce huge fantasy scores.
